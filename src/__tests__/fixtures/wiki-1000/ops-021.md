# Disaster Recovery Planning

## RPO and RTO

Every disaster recovery plan starts with two numbers:

- **RPO (Recovery Point Objective)**: how much data can you afford to lose? If RPO is 1 hour, you need backups at least every hour.
- **RTO (Recovery Time Objective)**: how long can the service be down? If RTO is 15 minutes, you need automated failover, not manual runbooks.

## DR Tiers

| Tier | Strategy | RPO | RTO | Cost |
|------|----------|-----|-----|------|
| 1 | Active-active multi-region | ~0 | ~0 | $$$$ |
| 2 | Warm standby | Minutes | Minutes | $$$ |
| 3 | Pilot light | Hours | 30-60 min | $$ |
| 4 | Backup and restore | Hours-days | Hours | $ |

## Our Approach

We run **Tier 2 (warm standby)** for critical services. A secondary region has infrastructure provisioned but scaled down. On failover, we scale up and switch DNS.

Database replication is continuous via **Aurora Global Database** with <1 second replication lag. S3 data uses **Cross-Region Replication**.

## DR Testing

Test DR quarterly. An untested DR plan is not a plan — it's a wish. Run **game days** where you simulate region failure and practice the failover procedure.
