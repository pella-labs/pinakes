# Incident Response

## Incident Severity Levels

| Level | Definition | Response Time | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| SEV1 | Complete outage or data loss | 5 min | Production database down |
| SEV2 | Major feature degraded | 15 min | Payment processing failing |
| SEV3 | Minor feature impacted | 1 hour | Search results slow |
| SEV4 | Cosmetic or low-impact | Next business day | Dashboard graph wrong |

## Incident Workflow

1. **Detect** — alert fires or user reports
2. **Triage** — assign severity, page on-call if SEV1/SEV2
3. **Communicate** — update status page, notify stakeholders
4. **Mitigate** — restore service (rollback, scale up, failover)
5. **Root cause** — investigate after service is restored
6. **Postmortem** — blameless review within 48 hours

## On-Call Expectations

- Acknowledge pages within 5 minutes
- Join the incident channel immediately
- Focus on mitigation first, root cause second
- Update the incident channel every 15 minutes during active incidents
- Hand off cleanly at shift change

## Postmortems

Every SEV1 and SEV2 gets a written **postmortem**. Focus on systems and processes, not people. Identify action items and track them to completion.
