# Access Review Process

Regular **access reviews** ensure that users retain only the permissions they need. Without reviews, permissions accumulate over time as people change roles.

## Review Frequency

- **Critical systems**: quarterly
- **Standard systems**: semi-annually
- **Low-risk systems**: annually
- **After role changes**: immediately

## What to Review

For each user or service account:

- Do they still need access to this system?
- Is their access level appropriate for their current role?
- Are there unused permissions that should be removed?
- Are there dormant accounts that should be deactivated?

## Process

1. Generate access reports from your identity provider
2. Assign reviews to system owners (not security team alone)
3. Owners approve or flag each access entry
4. Flagged access is reviewed with the user's manager
5. Unapproved access is revoked within a defined SLA
6. Document the review for compliance (see [[sec-022]])

## Automation

Reduce review burden through automation:

- Flag accounts unused for 90+ days
- Highlight permissions that exceed the standard set for a role
- Auto-revoke access after a defined period without re-certification
- Generate diff reports showing permission changes since last review

## Service Accounts

Service accounts are often forgotten in reviews. Include them explicitly:

- Who owns the service account?
- What does it access?
- When was the key last rotated? (see [[sec-016]])
- Is it still in active use?
