# Monitoring and Alerting

## The Three Pillars

Modern observability rests on three pillars:

- **Metrics** — numeric measurements over time (Prometheus, CloudWatch)
- **Logs** — discrete events with context (Loki, CloudWatch Logs, ELK)
- **Traces** — request flow across services (Jaeger, X-Ray, Tempo)

## Alerting Philosophy

Good alerts are **actionable**, **urgent**, and **real**. If an alert fires and the response is "ignore it," the alert is broken.

### Alert Levels

- **P1 Critical** — revenue or data loss, pages on-call immediately
- **P2 High** — degraded service, pages during business hours
- **P3 Medium** — anomaly detected, creates a ticket
- **P4 Low** — informational, Slack notification only

### Alert Fatigue

The number one observability anti-pattern is **alert fatigue**. Too many low-quality alerts train people to ignore them. Ruthlessly prune alerts that don't lead to action.

## Key Metrics to Monitor

For any web service, start with these:

- Request rate (throughput)
- Error rate (5xx percentage)
- Latency (p50, p95, p99)
- Saturation (CPU, memory, disk, connections)

This is the **RED method** combined with **USE method** for infrastructure resources.
