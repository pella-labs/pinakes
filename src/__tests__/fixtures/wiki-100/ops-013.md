# Incident Response Process

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Criteria | Response Time | Communication |
|----------|----------|---------------|---------------|
| SEV-1 | Complete service outage | 5 minutes | StatusPage, exec Slack |
| SEV-2 | Partial degradation, >5% users affected | 15 minutes | StatusPage, team Slack |
| SEV-3 | Minor issue, workaround available | 1 hour | Team Slack only |

## Incident Lifecycle

### 1. Detection

Incidents are detected through three channels: automated alerts (Prometheus/PagerDuty), customer reports, and internal discovery. Whoever detects the issue declares the incident in `#incidents` with the initial severity.

### 2. Response

The **on-call engineer** is the Incident Commander (IC) until they hand off. The IC's responsibilities:

- Coordinate responders
- Maintain the incident channel timeline
- Communicate status updates every 30 minutes
- Decide on escalation

### 3. Mitigation

Priority is restoring service, not finding root cause. Common mitigations:

- Rollback the last deployment
- Scale up replicas
- Toggle feature flags off
- Failover to standby region

### 4. Resolution

The incident is resolved when the SLI returns to within SLO thresholds for 15 consecutive minutes.

### 5. Post-Mortem

Every SEV-1 and SEV-2 gets a **blameless post-mortem** within 72 hours. The document covers: timeline, root cause, impact, what went well, what went poorly, and action items with owners and due dates.

Post-mortems are stored in the wiki under `incidents/` and linked from [[security-policies]].
