# On-Call Rotation

## Rotation Structure

The on-call rotation follows a **weekly schedule** with a primary and secondary on-call engineer. Handoff happens every Monday at 10:00 AM local time. The handoff includes a 30-minute sync between outgoing and incoming on-call to review:

- Active incidents or ongoing issues
- Any silenced alerts and their expiration
- Known upcoming risks (deployments, migrations, maintenance windows)

## Escalation Path

If the primary on-call doesn't acknowledge a page within 5 minutes:

1. PagerDuty escalates to the secondary on-call
2. After another 5 minutes, escalates to the engineering manager
3. After 15 minutes total, escalates to the VP of Engineering

## On-Call Expectations

- Acknowledge pages within 5 minutes during business hours, 15 minutes outside
- Maintain a laptop and internet connection during the on-call window
- Triage and mitigate SEV-1/SEV-2 incidents; document actions in the incident channel
- Write post-mortem drafts for any SEV-1 or SEV-2 incident that occurs during the rotation

## Compensation

On-call engineers receive additional compensation for each week on rotation. SEV-1 incidents outside business hours receive additional incident pay. The compensation details are in the HR wiki, not here.

## Burnout Prevention

No engineer is on-call more than one week per month. If the team is too small to support this rotation, the engineering manager escalates to hire. On-call load is reviewed quarterly as a team health metric.
